Ahh, there's nothing more relaxing than a sea day. Sleeping late, raiding the buffet, lounging on the deck. Why the possibilities are nearly endless!

We got to sleep late
in our staterooms.
There was no waking up early this day, everyone got to catch up on their sleep. Nine o'clock was the earliest people had risen. Some, praytell who, didn't rise til the bingo hour, which happened to be 11:30 a.m. on this ship. The family met for a buffet lunch on the Sun Deck after bingo, at which no one in our group won.

The rest of the afternoon was just a big chill-fest, nobody had anywhere they needed to be or anything they needed to do. TV and talking ruled this time. With seven o'clock came the usual cocktail hour. Dinner was excellent as usual, and some people watched the night's movie, while others headed straight to bed. All in all, everyone had a great day.

So much didn't happen this day, but thats the way it's supposed to be! If any more happened I would hesitate to call it a sea day! But anyway, you can either go forward to the day in Haifa written by Heidi, since I was sick and didn't leave the ship, or back to the day in Rhodes.
