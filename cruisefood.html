Ah, the cruise life. Exotic ports, luxurious surroundings and delicious food. Very delicious food. Be it morning breakfast-in-bed or a formal night feast, the wonderful cooks on the Pacific Princess are the best at what they do.

Nathan's appetizer: Escargot (double order) and pizza.
Every night the kitchen staff prepares more than enough food to feed an entire army. However, who they are preparing food for eat more than an army. Imagine one Nathan and the quantity of food he can consume, then multiply that by one thousand. Yessir, this ship's food storage takes up almost a third of a deck. With all the food these men have to mass-produce, you'd think it would be like your school cafeteria. No, no my friend, this is the best food you will ever taste.

A wonderful shrimp cocktail.
An appetizer that only
an adult would order.
Not only is the food the finest in the world, you can have as much of it as you want as well. That's right, all meals are prepaid, meaning you can eat like a bird or a horse, it doesn't matter. And the horses in the crowd have six courses to dine on: Appetizer, Soup, Salad, Pasta, Entree and Desert. Shown on the right are two selections from the appetizer portion of the menu. It is important to note as well that each and every night the menu in packed full of entirely new things to taste and fall in love with. There are however selections that are available every night; T-bone steak and Caesar salad to name a few. There is a full fledged vegetarian menu as well, along with all the wine you could ever drink which is recommended to you based on your tastes by the ship's sommelier.

A cornish game hen with mashed potatoes and mushrooms.
The name of our head waiter was Renzo, and he took very good care of us. The pizza seen in an earlier picture (above) was not available on the menu. Renzo had three to four of them specially prepared for our table each night, which was a big hit with the younger members of the group. He also got to the point that he could predict what you'd order off the menu. In Nathan's case, he'd usually add 'double order' to whatever he said.
