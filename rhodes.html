Rhodes was the last stop we were going to make in Greece before we ended the cruise in Athens. The town boasted a lot to see; the Church of Our Lady and the Chapel of St. George on Mount Philerimos, the old walled town of Rhodes and the extravagant Palace of the Grand Masters. All that and a walk around the harbor where the Colossus of Rhodes - one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World - stood astride its entrance spelled one interesting day.

Another tour day, another early morning. Everyone dressed, washed and breakfasted in time for the eight thirty a.m. bus departure. Our first stop: Mount Philerimos.

We arrived at the Church of Our Lady and proceeded to climb a terraced pathway surrounded by shrubs until we reached the first of the establishment's ruins. Yes the guide talked, but that's not what everyone had their eyes on. The show had been stolen, by none another that Mr. Lizard himself. The little fellow would come out from his crack in the rocks to sun himself, and dart back in when humans got too near. This continued until we advanced further into the complex.

The tour led us through a series of courtyards and hallways until we were given the option to roam freely, so we traveled toward the observation point, nearly 1,000 feet above sea level, from which excellent views were available. On the walk back to the bus a field full of sheep were visible, and provided another spark of amusement to the more dissuaded members of the Family Federation. After everyone piled into the bus we were off to our next destination, the 14th century Palace of the Grand Masters.

An extremely detailed mosaic
of many different fish.
The bus was parked a fair distance away from the palace, which gave us the opportunity to walk the cobblestone streets and take in much more of the architecture that surrounded us. Cats lounged about and came running to people for some petting or perhaps food. We finally came to the archway that we had to pass through

to enter the palace. Entering, we turned to the left and climbed a set of stairs to enter a giant room decorated with mosaics on the floor, walls and ceiling. The fish mosaic was one of these. Each room we went through was as elaborately decorated as the first, if not more so. When at last our tour of the inner palace came to a close, we were directed into the courtyard to admire the wall of the palace.

Our walk of the Old Town inevitably led to a shopping district, upon which the group skattered like mice after cheese into the surrounding shops. T-shirts, sunglasses, medicinal herbs, it was all there. The time came to leave and everyone reassembled and made their way for the bus.

The last segment of the tour was upon us. All we had remaining was a driving tour along the west coast. The pleasant scenery made this go by all the quicker. Before we knew it, the tour was over. Everyone spent the rest of the day either spending more time in Rhodes or returning to the ship to relax. The casual dress dinner made a perfect end to the already excellent day.
