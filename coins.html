Ancient coins are a treasure long sought after by pirates, rich private collectors and even imaginative youngsters. But how does an everyday doctor on vacation get his hands on eight rare Roman coins? Read on, and be enlightened.

Tim - public enemy number one.
Yes, its true. Remember Tim Anderson? I knew you would. As certain evidence has come to light, it seems that the pharmaceutical business isn't the only thing Tim is interested in. While the rest of the Family Federation was innocently strolling about St. John's Basilica, Tim was eyeing possible ancient Roman coins.
A fistful of these for a fistful of coins.
That's right, like the snake he is, Tim slithered into a dark passage to rendezvous with an equally vile creature, the coin pusher. In this misty, poorly lit crack-in-the-ground, Tim acquired a handful of ancient coins. Ancient coins or tourist fakes? We don't know, and I'll leave it up to you to draw your own conclusions.
